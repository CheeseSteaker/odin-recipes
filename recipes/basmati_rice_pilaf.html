Basmati Rice Pilaf

Description

A wonderful dish full of flavor that is sure to make its enjory to ask for a second bowl. This dish can either be eaten by it self, as just two serving of this rice can fill you, or as a diner, or can compliment another main entre as it pairs well with a chicken or beef.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups basmati rice
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped yellow onions
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cummin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 1/4 cups water
  • 1 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/3 cup dried fruit, such as currants, raisins or finely chopped dried apricots
  • 1/4 cup sliced or slivered almonds, toasted in a small dry skillet over medium heat until golden, about 5 minutes

Directions

  1. Place the rice in a medium bowl and add enough water to cover by 2 inches.
  2. Using your hands, gently swish the grains to release any excess starch.
  3. Carefully pour off the water, leaving the rice in the bowl.
  4. Repeat four to five times, until the water runs almost clear.
  5. Using a fine mesh strainer, drain the water from rice.
  6. Melt the butter in a pot over medium-low heat. Add the onion and cook, stirring regularly, until softened but not browned, about 4 minutes.
  7. Add the cumin, turmeric, cinnamon, and garlic and cook, stirring constantly, until fragrant, about 30 seconds longer.
  8. Add the rice to the pot, and cook, stirring constantly, for about 3 minutes.
  9. Add the water, salt, and pepper and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until all the liquid is absorbed, 15 to 18 minutes.
  10. Off the heat, remove the lid and sprinkle the dried fruit over the rice (do not mix in).
  11. Place the lid over the pot and let stand for 10 minutes. Toss in the toasted almonds, fluff the rice with a fork, and serve.
